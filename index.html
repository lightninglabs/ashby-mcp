Profound's Top Tactics

1

Category Creation + Founder Narrative

Repeatedly framing a new market so the company becomes synonymous with it. CEO James Cadwallader coined "Answer Engine Optimization" and drives it on every podcast, post, and partnership.
2

Original Research + Product Data

Publishing proprietary analysis that doubles as thought leadership and technical proof. 700K conversations analyzed, 26M prompts studied, 27M citations tracked — research gets cited by LinkedIn's own marketing team.
3

Launches + Fundraising as Brand Moments

Treating every milestone as a coordinated visibility event across channels. $96M raise became a Fortune exclusive, Nasdaq MarketSite celebration, Sequoia blog post, and social blitz — all at once.
4

Partnerships + Ecosystem Building

Using integrations, certifications, docs, and programs to expand the company's surface area. G2, Partnerize, Gamma, Nostra, Parallel AI, Notified, Avenue Z — each one a co-branded moment with blog, LinkedIn, and press.
5

Events + Distributed Team Visibility

Making the broader team public through events, speaking, and operator content — not just the CEO. Zero Click conference, AI Engineer Happy Hours, Nick Lafferty at SXSW, CTO Dylan Babbs on LinkedIn.

Key Takeaways

The Playbook for Your Brand

1

Update Careers Website & Page

The easiest quick win. Your careers page is the first thing candidates check — make it reflect your ambition.

2

Lead with the Founder

Founder visibility creates magnetic pull. Both Modal and Profound's CEOs are the face of their brands on podcasts and social.

3

Use Fundraising as a Brand Moment

Every milestone — fundraise, partnership, launch — should be a coordinated, multi-channel brand event.

4

Publish the Hard, Interesting Work

Technical deep-dives and original research prove your team solves real problems, attracting like-minded talent.

5

Build Community with Curated Events

Demo nights, conferences, happy hours — create face-to-face touchpoints that make cold outreach feel warm.

6

Use Partnerships & Customer Proof

Every partnership and customer story is a brand moment. Volume creates ambient awareness that compounds over time.

7

Reframe the Narrative

Don't compete in existing categories. Create your own framing — like "Answer Engine Optimization" — and own the conversation.

8

Get the Team Posting, Not Just the Company

When the whole team is publicly visible, candidates see who they'd work with. Distributed content > centralized marketing.
